## Episode 1 - Magical Girl

The episode begins with an introduction of the historical context: in the year 1939, entities given the name of Neuroi started appearing from dark, cloudy, vortex-like formations in the sky. Their origin or goals were unknown, but they started attacking humanity and destroying countries. As a countermeasure, humanity invented an anti-Neuroi weapon: the *Striker Unit*. It's a tool resembling a pair of torpedos that augments magical power and allows its user to fly in the sky by putting their legs inside them. Magic users called Witches were gathered from all over the world, and an elite task force was organized to fight the Neuroi: the 501st Joint Fighter Wing. Their members are called the "Strike Witches".

After the opening, we're shown a fight scene between this task force and a Neuroi. We are then introduced to our main character, **Miyafuji Yoshika**, climbing on a tree to try and rescue a cat. Later that day, as she and her friend **Michiko** are being given a ride back home by a local watermelon farmer, Yoshika recalls the disappearance of her father, leaving for his research work to fight the Neuroi only to never come back. At the same time, they are spotted by **Major Sakamoto Mio** and officer Hijikata. An animal runs on the road and the farmer tries to avoid it, sending the two girls flying. Michiko ends up gravely hurting herself, and Yoshika uses her magical power to try and heal her, with the support of Mio. She ends up faiting, and is brought home by the Major. Michiko is healed by her mother and grandmother, both magic users. As for Yoshika, Mio offers her to join the military to fight the Neuroi. She refuses, as she hates war.
The next day, she receives a letter from her father, who was supposed to be dead. One of the two pictures it contains features Mio standing next to her father, so she decides to go ask her if her dad is alive.

## Episode 2- That Which I Can Do

Intro summary: "The year 1944. the battle between humanity and the enigmatic Neuroi continued to intensify. Miyafuji Yoshika set out for Britannia alongside Sakamoto Mio in order to investigate her father's situation. Just as they thought the long voyage was finally over, the Neuroi appeared, pursuing her fleet. The 501st Joint Fighter Wing, a.k.a. Strike Witches, set out from Britannia to rescue them."

Yoshika, hiding in her cabin, her hands covering her ears, witnesses countless ships getting destroyed, planes being lost and men fighting for their lives. Mio gives her an intercom for them to communicate if needed, and then goes to join the fight. Yoshika feels powerless, and wonders how she could be of help. She lays her eyes on a bunch of medical supplies, and sets out to help people the best she can. She calls out to Mio, still fighting in the sky, who notices her outside and scolds her through the intercom, but is ultimately impressed by her guts.
A Neuroi attack hits a turrent and harms a marine, so Yoshika tries to heal him with her magic, but isn't able to control it well and is pushed aside by another marine who tells her not to get in the way. She expressed her willingness to help, so they ask her to go fetch bandages. As she does, the fight intensifies even more, and she gets caught in the despair filling the ship.

## Episode 4 - Thanks

However, Lynette would not open up to Yoshika. It was because she believed she was useless. But with Miyafuji's help, she attained her first war achievement. And so, Miyafuji made a new friend."

Trude has a dream about her past, when she fought a battle against a Neuroi to defend a city. She wakes up screaming the name of a little girl, Chris, presumably her little sister.
This morning, Yoshika and Lynne are preparing breakfast while chatting about the topic of saving everyone. Trude rejects it, saying it's impossible. Her friends join and notice something's not right. Trude quickly eats and leaves. Meanwhile, Perrine complains about the natto Yoshika prepared.
Later, while she's training, Mio and Wilcke discuss the matter and decide to try and pair her with Yoshika. Yoshika also manages to get Perrine drenched in water with her mop while cleaning.
Trude, back in her room, remembers the events of the past: Chris became unconscious, probably due to trauma, and showed no signs of waking up. A doctor took her with him on a boat to Britannia.
The next morning, while the girls have tea, Mio tells Yoshika to train together with Trude. Yoshika also starts slurping while drinking her tea (due to being Japanese), prompting once more the anger of Perrine. After that, Mio gives Trude and Yoshika their pay, which Trude asks them to do "the same as usual" with, meaning probably sending it to her sister or family. After that, in the bath, Yoshika discusses with Lynne how much the pay she just got is worth, comparing it to four thousands bowls of rice.
