Summary: Mahou Shoujo ni Akogarete

Aliases:

  • Gushing over Magical Girls

MAL: https://myanimelist.net/anime/54722/Mahou_Shoujo_ni_Akogarete

Episode 1: A Villainess Leader is Born?!

Magical girls named Tres Magia fight against Enormita, an evil organization trying to take over the world. Hiiragi Utena meets Venalita, a mascot of the evil organization. Claiming she has the power of a chosen one, he recruits her and gives her the power of transform. Utena refuses, but Venalita follows and tries to convince her. Utena is forced into a confrontation against Tres Magia and summons a flower monster that binds the magical girls. With a push from Venalita, she starts tormenting Tres Magia with her whip and takes pleasure in doing so, deluding herself into thinking it’s a dream. The next morning, the four girls go to school in the same class, oblivious to each other’s identity. Tres Magia suddenly sense an enemy and leave the class to fight it. With pressure from Venalita, Utena does the same and gets into a fight using mannequins. Her sadistic side surfaces once again as she tickles the magical girls, before escaping when they start breaking free. Back home, she begins to realize that she might feel pleasure from torturing her beloved idols.

Episode 2: Magia Baiser

At school, Utena daydreams about abusing Tres Magia. Concerned, Minakami Sayo (Magia Azure) asks her after school if she is okay. Utena stops by a shrine on the way back home to pray to be freed from her thoughts and situation, but stumbles upon a trail of erotic magazines on the way back. She follows the books placed there by Venalita, and ends up reading them. As she is learning about S&M, her powers overflow and are noticed by Sayo. She appears and confronts her, but Utena turns the situation around and tortures her with a blindfold. Utena runs away when the other girls show up, leaving Sayo conflicted about her feelings. Back at home, Venalita gets her to pick a name for herself, and makes up a lit about the reason he has her fighting Tres Magia. As for them, they have a meeting at a café to discuss the situation. Tenkawa Kaoruko (Magia Sulfur) notices Sayo is acting weird, and decides to bait Utena by going alone in an empty place. Utena shows up and attacks her with a candle monster, before Kaoruko turns the tide around and reveals her true power. Instead of a defensive shield, she uses huge offensive gloves to attack Utena. Before she runs away, she tells Kaoruko her name: Magia Baiser. While Utena is catching her breath in a back alley, a mysterious new girl observes her from above.

Episode 3: The Explosive Girl, Leopard

Venalita gives Utena a tour of Enormita’s headquarters, Nacht Base. There, she meets another member: Araga Kiwi (Leopard). Kiwi explains that she hates Magia Tres for stealing the spotlight from her, the cutest girl in the world. She then attacks her with explosives and weapons, but Utena turns things around by trapping Kiwi inside a lighbulb and zapping her. As she’s torturing Kiwi, she explains to her that she doesn’t want to fight anyone, but she loves seeing the new faces Tres Magia make every time. Kiwi falls for Utena when she tells her that right now, she is the cutest girl in the world. The next morning, Kiwi surprises Utena by revealing she is transferring into her school. Later, they meet outside of school to spend the day together. Kiwi suddenly transforms them both to take a picture, but Magia Tres shows up and a fight begins. Utena tortures Sayo again, then saves Kiwi who was facing both Haruka and Kaoruko.

Episode 4: The Strongest Idols <3 Tres Magia

Utena gets into a fight with Tres Magia but quickly runs away: her grades have been falling recently, so she needs to study for the make up exam. On the way back home, she finds Magia Magenta trying to taunt her. Venalita forces Utena to fight her, so she expedites the fight and goes back home. There, she struggles to study, her thoughts filled with Magia Magenta’s nude body. She goes back to fight her again and clear her mind. After an intense battle, Utena leaves and Sayo and Kaoruko find Haruka and ask her why she challenged Magia Baiser by herself. She replies that she wanted to confirm something. The day of the exam, Utena still hasn’t studied. She begs Kiwi to help her, and somehow manages to pass. The following day, Kiwi and Kaoruko get into a fight at school. Then, while Tres Magia were having a photoshoot for magazine, they get challenged by Utena and Kiwi. The two teams have an intense battle.

Episode 5: Neroalice in Wonderland

Utena and Kiwi meet their new teammate, Morino Korisu, a young girl that Venalita met in a back alley. Her power allows her to trap people in her doll houses and make them do what she wants. She uses it to trap Magia Tres and make them do obscene things to each other. The next day, Kiwi and Utena meet Korisu by chance and decide to go spend time at her place. There, Utena offers to repair her favorite doll. She spends the night doing so and catches a cold. Korisu uses her power to cure her cold.

Episode 6: Tres Magia’s Secret Backstory

Haruka spots and befriends Korisu while having fun with children at a park. Later, after a fight, Kaoruko mentions that Haruka acts like the group’s mom, which prompts her to start doing it actively (with bentos and general emotional support). After classes, she also spends time playing house with Korisu. After she leaves, Korisu gets sad, transforms into Neroalice and traps Magia Magenta inside of her doll house. She turns her into a baby and plays house with her. Meanwhile, Kaoruko reminisces about her first time meeting Haruka and Sayo, as well as the event which led to her joining their group. Magia Magenta also remembers her friends and breaks free from the mind control, but ends up going along with Neroalice’s act to make her happy. She keeps playing house while being an adolescent and fully aware of what is happening, and ends up peeing herself. Magia Azure and Magia Sulfur finally notice the enemy presence and rush to help her, but Neroalice is already gone. Haruka decides to continue acting as their mom.
